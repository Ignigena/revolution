Built-in iSight Workaround

For users with Apple's iMac, MacBook or MacBook Pro line of computers, getting live video to work can be a pain as your Mac will sometimes default to your built-in iSight camera. The steps below should allow you to consistently get your Mac to give ProWorship your FireWire or USB camera instead of the iSight.

To ignore your built-in iSight:

  

Open ProWorship and go to the Preferences window.

  

Under the "General" tab, select the "Disable" option under the "Built-in iSight Camera" section.

  

You will be prompted for your administrator name and password. ProWorship is simply modifying the permissions of certain system files. No essential system files are being deleted.

  

Restart ProWorship for the permissions changes to take effect.

  

Connect your Firewire or USB camera to the Mac and turn it on. Make sure the camera is in Record mode and not Playback.

  

Open up ProWorship and turn on live video. Your external camera should be accessed and your iSight ignored.

Note that during this time iChat, Photobooth, and other programs will be unable to access your iSight camera. You must go back into ProWorship, enable the camera, and relaunch any programs that need to use your iSight.

If you are running OS 10.4 you can try the following alternate approach which will keep your iSight accessable to iChat and other programs while giving ProWorship your external camera. (Note: this only works in OS 10.4. Users running 10.5 "Leopard" must follow the steps outlined above)

To ignore your built-in iSight:

  

Close ProWorship as well along with any other programs that may be accessing your camera (iChat, iMovie or Quicktime).

  

Connect your Firewire or USB camera to the Mac and turn it on. Make sure the camera is in Record mode and not Playback.

  

Open up ProWorship and turn on live video. Your external camera should be accessed and your iSight ignored.
